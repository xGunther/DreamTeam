Characters

The Lion King tells the story of Simba, a young lion prince who must overcome his past and claim his rightful place as king. Along the way, he meets an array of unforgettable characters, each with their own unique personalities and storylines. From the wise and powerful Mufasa to the hilarious duo of Timon and Pumbaa, the characters of The Lion King have become iconic in the world of animation. On this page we will explore the characters of The Lion King, their roles in the story, and the lasting impact they have had on audiences worldwide.

Simba

Simba is the central character in The Lion King. As a young cub, Simba is full of energy, mischief and curiosity, always eager to explore and learn about the world around him. However, when tragedy strikes, and he loses his father, Simba is left to face his own demons and doubts about his ability to be a good king. Over the course of the movie, we see Simba grow from a carefree cub to a wise and compassionate leader. Despite his past, he learns to embrace his responsibilities, face his fears, and ultimately becomes the true king of the Pride Lands. With his charming personality, infectious energy, and a fierce determination to do what's right, Simba has become an enduring symbol of hope and resilience for audiences of all ages. In the original movie the young version of Simba is voiced by Jonathan Taylor Thomas and the older verion of Simba is voiced by Matthew Broderick.

Nala

Nala is one of the most memorable characters in The Lion King. As a cub, she is Simba's best friend and partner in crime, always ready for an adventure. As an adult, Nala becomes a strong, determined and independent lioness, with a fierce loyalty to her pride and a deep love for Simba. She is unafraid to speak her mind and challenge the status quo, as we see when she goes in search of help to save the Pride Lands from Scar's tyranny. Nala's bravery and resourcefulness make her an integral part of the story, and her unwavering support helps Simba realize his true potential as a leader. With her adventurous spirit, quick wit, and undeniable courage, Nala has become an inspiring character for audiences of all ages. In the original movie Nala is voiced by Moira Kelly.

Timon and Pumbaa

Timon and Pumbaa are two of the most beloved and humorous characters in The Lion King. As unlikely friends, this duo brings a lighthearted and carefree energy to the movie. Timon, a wisecracking meerkat, and Pumbaa, a jovial warthog, are known for their witty banter, catchy tunes, and their unique approach to life. They teach Simba to live in the moment and to forget about his worries. Timon and Pumbaa also become mentors to Simba, providing him with the guidance and support he needs to become a wise and just king. Whether they are singing "Hakuna Matata" or providing comic relief during a tense moment, Timon and Pumbaa add a playful and entertaining dimension to The Lion King. With their infectious personalities and their unwavering loyalty to their friends, Timon and Pumbaa have become iconic characters, bringing laughter and joy to audiences of all ages. Nathan Lane voices Timon and Ernie Sabelle voices Pumbaa in the original movie.

Mufasa

Mufasa is one of the most iconic characters in the classic movie, The Lion King. As the king of the Pride Lands and Simba's father, Mufasa is revered for his wisdom, strength, and compassion. He is a true leader, who is respected by all the animals in the kingdom. Mufasa is fiercely protective of his family and his pride, always putting their needs above his own. He is also a mentor to Simba, teaching him about the Circle of Life, and preparing him to be a great king. Mufasa's tragic death at the hands of his brother Scar is one of the most heart-wrenching moments in the movie, and his legacy lives on in Simba's journey to become a leader. With his commanding presence, powerful voice, and unwavering moral compass, Mufasa has become a symbol of strength, wisdom, and love for audiences of all ages. Mufasa is voiced by James Earl Jones.

Scar

Scar is one of the most notorious and memorable villains in the classic movie, The Lion King. As Mufasa's younger brother, he is jealous of his brother's power and craves the throne for himself. He is a cunning and manipulative lion who will stop at nothing to achieve his goals. Scar is a master of deception, using his wit and intelligence to trick Simba into leaving the Pride Lands and later, to turn the lionesses against each other. Scar is willing to go to any length to gain power, including killing his own brother and attempting to kill Simba. Scar's ruthless nature and his disregard for the Circle of Life make him a truly terrifying antagonist. With his sharp tongue, his distinctive voice, and his menacing presence, Scar has become one of the most iconic and memorable Disney villains of all time. Scar is voiced by Jeremy Irons.

Zazu

Zazu is one of the most memorable and amusing characters in the classic movie, The Lion King. As a hornbill bird, he is the majordomo and trusted advisor to Mufasa and later, to Simba. Zazu is prim and proper, and takes his duties very seriously. He is known for his dry sense of humor, his pedantic nature, and his comically British accent. Despite his often uptight personality, Zazu is fiercely loyal to the royal family and the Pride Lands, always putting their interests first. He is also highly intelligent, often providing valuable advice and insights to the king and prince. Zazu's hilarious antics and his distinctive personality make him an entertaining and beloved character in The Lion King. With his wit, his humor, and his unwavering loyalty, Zazu has become an iconic character, adding a playful and entertaining dimension to the movie. Zazu is voiced by the famous actor Rowan Atkinson.

Rafiki

Rafiki is one of the most enigmatic and wise characters in the classic movie, The Lion King. As a baboon, he is the shaman and spiritual leader of the Pride Lands. Rafiki is known for his unconventional ways, often speaking in riddles and using unorthodox methods to get his point across. He is a sage, with a deep understanding of the Circle of Life, and he helps guide Simba on his journey to become a leader. Rafiki is fiercely loyal to the Pride Lands, and he is not afraid to stand up against Scar's tyranny. With his mystical qualities, his whimsical personality, and his iconic catchphrase "Asante sana, squash banana," Rafiki has become one of the most beloved characters in The Lion King. He is a source of wisdom and inspiration, offering valuable life lessons and advice to audiences of all ages. Whether he is painting a portrait of Simba or leading a rousing chorus of "He Lives in You," Rafiki brings a sense of magic and wonder to the movie. Rafiki is voiced by Robert Guillaume.

Shenzi, Banzai, and Ed

Shenzi, Banzai, and Ed are a trio of villainous hyenas who play a pivotal role in the classic movie, The Lion King. Shenzi is the leader of the pack, with a fierce and cunning personality. Banzai is the second in command, known for his brash and impulsive nature. Ed, on the other hand, is a comically dim-witted hyena, who rarely speaks and communicates mostly through laughter. Together, they form a menacing and dangerous group, serving as Scar's henchmen and carrying out his orders. The hyenas are known for their viciousness, their unyielding loyalty to Scar, and their hatred for the lion pride. Shenzi, Banzai, and Ed are the primary antagonists of the movie, and they are responsible for many of the dark and dangerous moments in the film. With their distinctive personalities and their ferocious nature, Shenzi, Banzai, and Ed have become iconic characters in The Lion King, adding a sense of danger and tension to the story. Shenzi is voiced Whoopi Goldberg, Banzai is voiced by Ceech Marin and Ed is voiced by Jim Cummings.

Sarabi

Sarabi is one of the most important and respected characters in the classic movie, The Lion King. As Mufasa's mate and Simba's mother, she is the queen of the Pride Lands and a respected member of the pride. Sarabi is known for her strength, her dignity, and her unwavering devotion to her family and her community. She is a wise and compassionate lioness, who plays an important role in Simba's journey to become a leader. Sarabi is fiercely protective of her son, and she is not afraid to stand up against Scar's tyranny, even when it puts her own life at risk. Sarabi's character is a symbol of strength, wisdom, and resilience, and she is a powerful example of a female character who stands up for what is right. With her commanding presence and her regal demeanor, Sarabi has become an iconic and beloved character in The Lion King, inspiring generations of viewers with her courage and her wisdom. Sarabi is voiced by Madge Sinclair.

Sarafina

Sarafina is a minor character in the classic movie, The Lion King. She is one of the lionesses in the pride and is Nala's mother. Sarafina is known for her beauty, her grace, and her fierce loyalty to her community. While her role in the movie is relatively small, Sarafina's character adds depth to the story, showing the importance of family, community, and the Circle of Life. She is a loving and protective mother, who cares deeply for her daughter and the other lion cubs in the pride. Sarafina's character is a reminder of the importance of the female lionesses in the pride, who play an essential role in maintaining the balance of the ecosystem. While she may not be one of the most prominent characters in The Lion King, Sarafina's character adds an important layer to the story, highlighting the beauty and strength of the animal kingdom. Sarafina is voiced by Zoe Leader.
